About This Timeline

This page presents a chronological timeline of the key events in the creation and early history of the United States Marine Corps (USMC), from its founding in 1775 through its reestablishment and early 19th-century developments. Each entry highlights significant moments, battles, and changes that shaped the Marine Corps.

Color Representation:
Blue blocks: USMC years as the Continental Marines during the Revolutionary War.
Orange blocks: The reestablishment of the modern USMC and its early history.

3 March 1776

The New Providence Raid, First Amphibious Operation

In an attempt to gain gunpowder for Washington's army. Commodore Hopkins took his men down to New Providence in the Bahamas seeking supplies. He gave Captain Samuel Nicholas the task of leading a raid on Fort Nassau, when met with opposition, Nicholas changed course to Fort Montague. At Fort Montague, Nicholas was met with little to no resistance, and was able to take the fort and its supplies. However, the night before the raid, the British had moved most of the gunpowder out of the fort, so the raid was not as successful as it could have been.

(Reference 1)

5 September 1776

The Naval Committee released the first official uniform for the Continental Marines.

Green coats with white facings, a round hat with the left brim turned up, and a leather stock worn around the neck. Marines were nicknamed "Leathernecks" due to the leather stock worn around the neck.

(Reference 1)

December 1776 - January 1777

The Battle of Trenton and Princeton

Proving to be an amphibious force, the Continental Marines were reported to be attached to Col. John Cadwalader's Pennsylvania militia and aided in local operations.

During this time Washington's famous crossing of the Delaware River took place capturing the Hessian garrison north of Trenton.

(Reference 1)

September 1777

Marines aid in the evacuation of a Pennsylvania militia garrison from Billingsport.

(Reference 1)

July 1779

Penobscot Bay

Under the command of Commodore Saltonstall, and in a joint operation with other military forces the Continental Marines were sent to Penobscot Bay to capture Fort George which is under British control. After a series of failed attempts to capture the fort, Saltonstall ordered "All ships fend for yourself (Reference 1)." He then proceed in ramming his ship into the bank and setting it on fire. All American military personal had to flee through the wilderness. Saltonstall was later court-martialed for his actions.

(Reference 1)

September 3 1783

Great Britain recognized the United States as a sovereign and independent power. The Revolutionary War was officially over.

(Reference 1)

3 June 1785

Congress authorized the selling of the decommissioned Alliance frigate, the last vessel of the Continental Navy. By this time the Continental Marines were already disbanded (Reference 1).

11 July 1798

Reestablishment of the United States Marine Corps

Due to ongoing tensions with Revolutionary France the United States Marine Corps was reestablished by an act of Congress, which authorized the formation of a Marine Corps to serve as a naval infantry force. This marked the beginning of the modern USMC.

(Reference 1)

August 1798

New Uniforms

The Marine Corps adopted a new uniform left over from Wayne's Legion, which included a blue coat with red facings. This uniform resembles the "dress blues" worn by Marines today.

(Reference 1)

June 1800

Napoleon Bonaparte's rise to power in France and calls for the war that was never declared to end.

(Reference 1)

21 August 1800

The Marine Corps' band, the Marine Band, gave its first public performance.

(Reference 1)

1801

New Years Day, the Marine Corps Band played at the President's House for the first time.

March 1801, the Marine Band played at the inauguration of President Thomas Jefferson and from then on would play at every presidential inauguration, becoming the "President's Own".

(Reference 1)

1801 - 1805

The First Barbary War

Yusef Karamanli, proclaims hiself Bey of Tripoli and in May 1801 declares War on the United States by cutting down the flagstaff of the US Consulate in Tripoli.
The USMC was sent to the Mediterranean and by 27 April 1805, the Marines have assualted the city of Derna and captured the palace.
3 June 1805, a treaty was signed with Yusef Karamanli, ending the war.
From the war Marine Lt. Presley O'Bannon comes back with a Mameluke sword, which is still worn by Marine officers today.

(Reference 1)

March 1804

The Marine Corps adopted a new uniform, blue coat with red facings, white pantalons, black leather calf boots, and a red plum on the top of the hat.
